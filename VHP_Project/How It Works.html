1. Casting.

 

Locate a vet. (How to locate a veteran)

Explain project and schedule a time/quiet place for the interview.

Get some background about war(s), branch, rank, duties, etc. so you can prepare ( Background information on wars ).

 

 

 

2. Pre-production.

 

Get to know the VHP App and select great questions from menu.

You can also write your own. Practice using the App to record and insert tags.

Bring tissues, notepaper, and a pen to the interview.

 

 

3. Lights, camera…

 

Find a quiet, well-lit room to record a 30+ minute interview.

Check sound; Make sure your phone is fully charged, in airplane mode, and on tripod/table;

Sit close to and remind vets they can skip any questions/stop at any point;

On App, complete fields and 3 required forms (bio data, veteran and interviewer release)

 

 

 

4. Action!

 

Start recording the interview.

Tap on the red notebook icon to insert tags to interesting/noteworthy parts of the interview.

 

.

 

 

5. Post-production.

 

Thank vet and ask if they have any questions or concerns and if they want to donate any original photos, letter, or other written material to the VHP archives.

Take pictures of the vet any original material.

Send thank you email with photos of vet’s original material.

Complete the required Video Recording log.

 

 

6. Distribution

Upload to the server : the video, video recording log, required forms, and photo log/manuscript data sheet (if relevant).

Copy all files to a thumb drive, include note identifying you/vet and mail it (along with any original photos or documents) to:

Veterans History Project
Library of Congress
101 Independence Ave., S
Washington, DC 20540-4615

 

 

7. Boom. Done.

You’ve just added a veteran’s invaluable story to the permanent archives of the Library of Congress!

Need more info? Visit http://www.loc.gov/vets/
